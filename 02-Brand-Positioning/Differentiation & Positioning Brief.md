# Differentiation / Positioning Brief — TDV Hackathon Site
*Synthesized from: TDV Competitor & Positioning Analysis + TDV Brand Fact Sheet*

## 1. The One-Sentence Answer

> **"Global consultancies plan and hand off. Global dev shops build what they're told. Terra's own team does both — strategy and delivery, one team, no handoff."**

**Compressed for hero use (≤6 words):**
> **"Strategy that ships."**

### Why this sentence and not a longer one
Refactoring UI's own principle applies here: hierarchy demands one dominant claim, not three competing ones. Every candidate sentence was tested against a single filter — *does it survive being read in 2 seconds on a phone, and is it falsifiable (i.e., not something every competitor could also claim)?*

| Rejected candidate | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "End-to-end strategy, technology, and data consulting" | This is literally Terra's current site copy — and it's also Accenture's, Deloitte's, and Capgemini's claim. Zero differentiation. |
| "Thailand's most influential advisor on digital venture building" | Unsubstantiated superlative (flagged in Brand Fact Sheet); invites the judge's obvious follow-up: "says who?" |
| "One team, three disciplines" | Descriptive, not contrastive — states what Terra has, not what's wrong with the alternative. |
| **"Strategy that ships."** | Passes: implies a criticism of the market (most strategy *doesn't* ship) without naming a competitor. Matches the pattern Refactoring UI calls "emphasize by de-emphasizing" — you don't need to shout louder than Accenture, you need Accenture's model to look worse by contrast. |

## 2. The Uncomfortable Truth This Claim Has to Survive

Per your operating instructions — holding nothing back: **"integration" is not a differentiator anymore.** The Competitor Analysis found that every major global firm (Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey, BCG, IBM, Capgemini, PwC, EY, the India majors) is actively converging strategy and delivery. Claiming "we do both" alone puts Terra in the same category as firms with 100,000x the headcount — a fight Terra loses instantly on scale and brand.

**The differentiation is not the claim "we do both." It's *why the giants structurally can't do it as well* — and Terra's proof has to lean on that structural gap, not on breadth.**

| Structural weakness in giants (from Competitor Analysis) | Terra's structural counter |
|---|---|
| Billable-hour economics reward *separating* strategy from execution and lengthening timelines | Terra's own About Us copy: *"We form long-term partnerships with our clients – to build, manage, and hand over solutions successfully"* — one team, not a handoff between practices |
| McKinsey/BCG explicitly hand off build to "implementation partners or client teams" | Terra's founding story: firm was created *because* founders "observed gaps... where real impact is hard to drive when there is lack of implementation support" |
| Senior rates $400–700/hr, enterprise-only, 9–24 month advisory cycles | Terra's case study proof: SEC-approved crypto platform built and activated in **6 months** |
| ~70–88% of large transformations fail (McKinsey/Bain's own research) — failure rooted in the strategy-execution gap | Terra's model is designed as the direct counter to that specific, named failure mode |

This is the actual first-scroll argument: **not "we're comprehensive," but "the gap that kills 70% of transformations is exactly the gap we don't have."**

## 3. Proof Stack — Terra Does Both (Not Just Claims It)

A claim without proof on first scroll is just marketing copy — indistinguishable from what's already on the current site. Judges score "UX" and "Business Story" partly on whether the differentiation is *demonstrated*, not just stated. Two proof tracks, sourced from the Brand Fact Sheet:

### Track A — Evidence of Consulting/Strategy Capability
| Proof Point | Source |
|---|---|
| Founders have "20+ years experience individually and 75+ years combined" in business management consulting | About Us |
| Explicit advisory function: *"our advisory team who will help define your ambitions and develop innovative solutions"* | About Us |
| Strategy service breadth: Digital Transformation, M&A/PMI, Corporate finance, ESG, Public Policy — genuine boardroom-level scope, not just IT strategy | Services page |

### Track B — Evidence of Technology/Delivery Capability (the part competitors can't match at this speed)
| Proof Point | Source |
|---|---|
| **SEC-approved crypto trading platform — activated and developed in 6 months**, integrated to global exchange, blockchain wallet, payment gateway, e-KYC | Case study #1 |
| End-to-end ICO platform with digital asset exchange integration | Case study #2 |
| Real-time, production-grade systems across solar monitoring, CRM, EV charging, loyalty architecture (incl. AI) | Case studies #5, #6, #7, #9 |
| Manufacturing capacity models and production planning tools shipped and in use ("improve stock levels and increase annual sales") | Case studies #11, #12 |

### ⚠️ Critical Gap — Flagging This Now, Not After Launch
The case studies prove **delivery** convincingly. They do **not** currently show the **strategy work that preceded each build** — none of the 12 blurbs mention the upstream strategic diagnosis, business case, or advisory engagement that led to the technology. This is the single biggest risk to the "Strategy that ships" claim: as written, the portfolio reads as a technology delivery shop with a consulting page bolted on, which is exactly the perception this positioning is trying to kill.

**Action required (not optional):** For at least 2–3 hero case studies on the new site, add a "Strategy → Delivery" framing — e.g., *"Diagnosed the compliance and market-entry challenge → Designed the operating model → Built and shipped the SEC-approved platform in 6 months."* Without this, the proof stack undercuts its own headline.

## 4. First-Scroll Message Architecture

Direct mapping to the mandatory hackathon requirement: *"ภายในส่วนแรกของหน้าเว็บไซต์ (First Scroll) ผู้เข้าชมต้องเข้าใจว่าเราแตกต่างจากคู่แข่งอย่างไร... ต้องสะท้อนให้เห็นว่าเราเป็นบริษัทที่ทำได้ทั้ง Strategic Consulting และ Technology Delivery"*

| Element | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Headline** | "Strategy that ships." | The one-sentence differentiator, compressed |
| **Subheadline** | "Terra combines strategic consulting and technology delivery in one team — so transformation doesn't stall between the deck and the deployment." | States both halves (Strategy + Delivery) explicitly — satisfies the mandatory requirement in words, not just implication |
| **Immediate proof strip (below the fold-line, still first scroll)** | 3 metrics/logos side by side, e.g. "6-month SEC-approved platform build" / "75+ years combined consulting experience" / client logo strip | Converts the claim from assertion to evidence within the same viewport — judges shouldn't have to scroll to section 4 to believe the headline |
